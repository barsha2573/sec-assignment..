Voices of the Hills through Literature

1. When the River Sleeps by Easterine Kire

This novel intertwines Naga folklore with environmental themes, portraying a solitary hunter’s spiritual journey through the forests of Nagaland. It highlights the deep connection between the Naga people and nature, emphasizing indigenous ecological knowledge.

2. A Terrible Matriarchy by Easterine Kire

Through the lens of a young girl’s experiences, this novel explores gender roles within a matrilineal Naga society, shedding light on the complexities of women’s lives and their relationships with cultural traditions.

3. Son of the Thundercloud by Easterine Kire

Blending myth and reality, this narrative centers on a woman’s role as a life-giver and spiritual guide, reflecting ecological feminism and the sacredness of nature in Naga culture.

4. These Hills Called Home: Stories from a War Zone by Temsula Ao

A collection of short stories that delve into the impact of political conflict on Naga society, particularly focusing on women’s experiences and their resilience amidst turmoil.

5. Mari by Easterine Kire

Set during World War II, this novel narrates the true story of a young Naga woman, capturing the intersection of personal and historical narratives, and highlighting women’s roles during times of upheaval.
